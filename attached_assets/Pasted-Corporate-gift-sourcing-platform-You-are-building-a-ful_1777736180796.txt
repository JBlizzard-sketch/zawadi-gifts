Corporate gift sourcing platform


You are building a full production-grade B2B corporate gifting platform specialising in premium Kenyan-made products. Read the full brief, expand the vision, present ~20 substantial phases, wait for "next."
The pain:
Every corporate in Nairobi — banks, telcos, multinationals, NGOs, law firms — spends money on end-of-year gifts, client appreciation hampers, staff onboarding kits, and event giveaways. The procurement manager Google searches "corporate gifts Nairobi," calls three suppliers in Industrial Area, and ends up with the same cheap imported mugs, pens, and calendars that everyone else buys. There is no curated platform offering premium, genuinely Kenyan-made products — Kenyan coffee, honey, leather goods, handmade ceramics, artisan chocolate, Maasai-inspired jewellery, natural skincare — with bulk pricing, branded packaging options, and reliable delivery. The artisans and small producers making beautiful things have no B2B sales channel. The corporates with real budgets have no quality option. This is a gap with money on both sides.
What you are building:
A curated B2B marketplace. Corporates browse curated gift collections by occasion (client gifts, staff appreciation, event giveaways, festive hampers), budget, and recipient profile. They configure their order — quantity, branded packaging with company logo, personalised message cards — and get a quote instantly. Orders above a threshold are handled by a dedicated account manager. The platform sources from vetted Kenyan artisans and small producers, manages quality control, handles packaging and logistics, and invoices the corporate on NET30 terms where appropriate. Producers get a reliable B2B sales channel they've never had.
Critical nuances:
Curation is the brand — every product on the platform must be genuinely good quality and genuinely Kenyan-made. A rigorous supplier onboarding process is essential. Branded packaging is the biggest value-add for corporates and must be handled smoothly — lead times communicated clearly, artwork specs provided upfront. Corporate procurement requires proper invoicing, KRA PIN numbers, and sometimes LPO (local purchase order) compatibility. The platform must handle mixed orders — a hamper with coffee from Kirinyaga, honey from Baringo, and chocolate from Nairobi — as a single order with a single delivery. The festive season (November–December) will be 60% of annual volume — the platform must be built to handle that surge.
Expand this. Think about: a "build your own hamper" configurator; a gift personalisation engine where each recipient in a list of 200 gets a personalised message card automatically; a producer stories feature that lets the corporate share who made the gift (huge ESG/sustainability angle); an annual gifting calendar tool that reminds procurement managers of upcoming occasions; a white-label option for large companies wanting a branded internal gift shop for staff; and a retail-facing version for individual buyers at premium price points.
Hosting: Next.js on Vercel. Backend on Railway. Product catalogue and order management in PostgreSQL. Image assets on Cloudinary or similar. Docker-ready for scale.
